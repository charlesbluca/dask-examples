        label='ROC Curve (area = {:.2f})'.format(auc(fpr, tpr)))

ax.set(

    ylim=(0, 1),
    title="ROC Curve",
    xlabel="False Positive Rate",
    ylabel="True Positive Rate",
)
ax.legend();
plt.show()

This Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curve tells how well our classifier is doing. We can tell itâ€™s doing well by how far it bends the upper-left. A perfect classifier would be in the upper-left corner, and a random classifier would follow the diagonal line.

The area under this curve is area = 0.76. This tells us the probability that our classifier will predict correctly for a randomly chosen instance.
